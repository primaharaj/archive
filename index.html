003 // Background

Finance was always the plan. Then I learnt how it really worked and the systems that prop it up, and the plan evolved. Most plans do when you start paying attention.

What replaced it was more interesting. The question of how capital moves, who it reaches, and whether the outcomes it claims to produce are real or just correlated with things that were already happening.

That question lives in credit markets, in climate finance, in development economics. Anywhere the measurement problem is hard and someone is making consequential decisions anyway.

The goal is to use the math behind the models to interrogate the systems, not just serve them.
